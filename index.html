About

My Introduction

I’m Mounira Zitouni, an ML engineer and applied AI researcher passionate about building scalable, production-ready machine learning systems. My work spans large language models, retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) over Neo4j knowledge graphs, and online learning frameworks that adapt under drift and severe class imbalance. I co-authored a peer-reviewed paper on LLM-driven specification generation, and I’ve engineered data pipelines that process millions of records with PyTorch, TensorFlow, and River. Beyond research, I’ve shipped automation and backend services as an SRE — from Google Docs API bots to CI/CD pipelines on Azure and GCP. I focus on building ML that is not just accurate, but reliable, explainable, and deployable at scale.

Research

Publications & in-progress work

Towards the LLM-Based Generation of Formal Specifications from Natural-Language Contracts

Venue: RAISE 2025 (Requirements Engineering for AI-powered Software) — Published
Google Scholar Link: arXiv:2411.15898
Authors: Mounira Zitouni et al.

Method to translate legal contracts into machine-readable Symboleo specs using optimized GPT-4o prompts; achieved ~70% faster creation than manual authoring.

Online Learning from Doubly Evolving Streams Susceptible to Class Imbalance

Status: Master’s Thesis (in progress), University of Ottawa
Focus: feature evolution, concept drift (incl. imbalance drift), adaptive classifiers & metrics.

Framework combining latent feature mapping, drift detectors (DDM/ADWIN) and imbalance-aware learners; evaluated with prequential F1, G-mean, AUC-PR and adaptation time.
